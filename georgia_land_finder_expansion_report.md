## 3. Opportunity Analysis

### A. Best Low-Risk Opportunities
These listings represent the safest investments due to **cleared titles** (via Land Bank mechanisms) and **existing utility access** with no flood risk:

* **GA-001 & GA-002 (Atlanta - MALB)**: The gold standard. Fully extinguished delinquent taxes and liens. Conveyed with a clear title from the LBA board after application. Pricing negotiated at nominal levels ($500 - $15,000) for affordable projects.
* **GA-034 (Macon - 884 Walnut St)**: A flat downtown historic infill lot with all utilities. Since it is managed by the Macon LBA, the board guarantees title and clears taxes. Immediate construction path.
* **GA-035 (Athens - 112 Rocksprings St)**: Walking distance to the University of Georgia campus. Extremely rare residential lot at $4,000, fully cleared by the Athens-Clarke County Land Bank.

### B. Best High-Upside Risky Opportunities
Opportunities with strong commercial potential but high title/redemption risk:

* **GA-044 (Atlanta - Fulton County Repository Lot)**: Priced at $3,500. This lot sat through two tax auctions and went to the County Repository list. The county deed is weak, but there is no 12-month redemption risk since the tax foreclosure is complete. Requires a **Quiet Title Action** (~$3,500) to secure insurable title, but yields a prime Atlanta intown lot for under $8,000 total.
* **GA-019 (Atlanta/DeKalb - Candler Park adjacent)**: A tax deed scheduled for sheriff sale in a highly desirable neighborhood. Starting bid of ~$15,000 on a lot worth $100K. Risk includes a **12-month redemption right** by the owner (which yields a guaranteed 20% penalty return to the buyer if redeemed) and the need for quiet title foreclosure.

### C. Listings to Avoid (Avoid Flag = Yes)
Properties flagged for severe structural or physical due-diligence failures:

* **GA-027 (GovDeals - Non-Standard Lot)**: Checked for irregular shapes and easement blocking.
* **GA-040 (Brunswick - 802 Lee St)**: High-risk Zone AE coastal floodplain. Requires elevated pier/crawlspace construction which increases foundation budgets by $20,000 - $40,000, negating the $4,500 low land price.
* **Avoid general tax deeds that are landlocked**: Checked county GIS records; any sheriff sale listing indicating no public right-of-way easement must be avoided as easement lawsuits are long and expensive.
