## Overview

This summary covers **30 documented legal land acquisition opportunities** across Georgia, focusing on Atlanta and metro counties. Properties were sourced exclusively from official government programs: land banks, tax sales, municipal surplus sales, and government-affiliated auctions. No adverse possession or illegal acquisition strategies are included.

All 30 entries represent **program-level listings** tied to verified official sources. Because land bank portals and tax sale lists update continuously, specific parcel addresses and IDs must be confirmed by visiting the live portals at the time of acquisition. The CSV notes exactly where to verify each listing.

## Recommended Next Steps Before Contacting Agencies or Bidding

### Step 1: Visit Live Portals (Do This First)
- **MALB:** [malb.epropertyplus.com](https://malb.epropertyplus.com) → Filter: Status = "Available", Property Type = "Residential Vacant"
- **DRLBA:** [dekalb-county-land-bank-ga-publicity.tolemi.com](https://dekalb-county-land-bank-ga-publicity.tolemi.com)
- **Clayton County Land Bank:** [claytoncountyga.gov](https://www.claytoncountyga.gov) → search "Land Bank"
- **Macon Land Bank:** [maconlandbank.org](https://maconlandbank.org) → subscribe to auction email list

### Step 2: Check Upcoming Tax Sale Lists
- **Fulton County:** Watch *South Fulton Neighbor* newspaper 4 weeks before each month's first Tuesday; check [fultoncountyga.gov](https://fultoncountyga.gov) Sheriff's Office Tax Sales page
- **DeKalb County:** Watch *The Champion* newspaper; check [dekalbtax.org](https://dekalbtax.org)
- **Clayton County:** Check [claytoncountyga.gov](https://www.claytoncountyga.gov) Tax Commissioner page
- **Cobb County:** Check [cobbtax.org](https://cobbtax.org) for Tax Sale Booklet
- **Gwinnett County:** Check [gwinnetttaxcommissioner.com](https://gwinnetttaxcommissioner.com) and *Gwinnett Daily Post*

### Step 3: For Each Identified Parcel – Verify These Before Proceeding
1. **FEMA Flood Zone:** [msc.fema.gov](https://msc.fema.gov) → enter address → confirm not in Zone AE or AH
2. **Zoning:** City of Atlanta Zoning Viewer or County GIS
3. **Parcel Ownership and Tax Status:** [fultonassessor.org](https://fultonassessor.org) (Fulton), [qpublic.net](https://qpublic.net) (DeKalb/Clayton), [cobbtax.org](https://cobbtax.org) (Cobb)
4. **Lien Search:** Request from county Superior Court Clerk
5. **Existing Conditions:** Drive by the parcel; note road frontage, utilities, and any structural issues
6. **Zoning Build Requirements:** Confirm minimum lot width, setbacks, and allowable use

### Step 4: For Land Bank Applications – Prepare These
- Written development proposal (what you intend to build, timeline, estimated cost)
- Proof of financing (bank statement, pre-approval letter, or equity commitment)
- References (2 business references)
- Clean tax record (no delinquent taxes on any property you own in the county)

### Step 5: For Tax Sales – Hire an Attorney First
- Georgia real estate attorneys familiar with quiet title actions: search Georgia State Bar at [gabar.org](https://www.gabar.org)
- Budget $1,500–$5,000+ for quiet title action post-acquisition
- Do not make improvements during the 12-month redemption period

## Important Legal Notes

> [!WARNING]
> **Georgia Tax Deed = Defeasible Title Only.** Purchasing a tax deed in Georgia does NOT give you immediate, marketable title. The original owner has **12 months** to redeem the property by paying your bid plus a **20% premium**. You cannot take possession, make improvements, or evict anyone during this period. A quiet title action is required after the redemption period to obtain marketable fee simple title.

> [!NOTE]
> **Land bank acquisitions are significantly safer** than tax sales because: (1) The land bank extinguishes delinquent taxes before conveying title, (2) There is no redemption period, (3) You receive a warranty or special warranty deed, (4) The land bank has typically screened for major environmental and title issues. The tradeoff is a more involved application process and affordable housing requirements on many parcels.

> [!TIP]
> **Best strategy for first-time Atlanta land acquisition:** Start with the MALB ePropertyPlus portal. Search for available vacant residential lots in your target neighborhood. Submit an application with a simple development proposal. MALB staff are accessible at (404) 525-9336 and can help guide you through the process. This path has the lowest legal risk and most transparent process.
